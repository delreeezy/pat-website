Introduction

AI is increasingly used to grade, monitor, and predict student performance. It offers efficiency and consistency, but raises serious ethical concerns about fairness and privacy. This site summarises my investigation into how AI assessment can be implemented responsibly in South African schools.

Focus question:
What are the key ethical considerations that should guide the implementation of AI for student assessment in the education and training sector?

Ethical principles for AI assessment

  • Fairness & bias prevention: Diverse data, bias testing, and inclusive design.
  • Privacy & data security: POPIA compliance, minimisation, secure storage.
  • Transparency & explainability: Clear purpose, user-facing explanations.
  • Accountability & human oversight: Appeal pathways and human-in-the-loop.
  • Reliability & safety: Ongoing monitoring, audit trails, fallback procedures.

Intended audience

Teachers, learners, and school administrators who need practical, ethical guidance for AI-based assessment in South African schools.
