Key findings

  1. Fairness and bias prevention are essential. AI can support fairer assessment by focusing on learning outcomes; however, without bias audits and inclusive design, it can reinforce inequities.
  2. Data privacy and accountability must be prioritised. POPIA-aligned practices, informed consent, and clear roles ensure responsible data handling and build trust.
  3. Clear policy and stakeholder involvement are vital. Fewer than 10% of institutions have formal policies on generative AI; involving educators, learners, and parents strengthens transparency and acceptance.

Conclusions

  • AI in assessment is acceptable to learners when it demonstrably supports learning and fairness.
  • Strong policies, privacy safeguards, and human oversight are essential to maintain trust and prevent harm.

Recommendations

  • Policy: Adopt a school AI policy that defines purpose, roles, appeal routes, and documentation.
  • Privacy: POPIA compliance, data minimisation, retention limits, and secure storage; obtain informed consent.
  • Fairness: Conduct regular bias audits; use diverse training data and inclusive validation.
  • Transparency: Provide student‑friendly explanations and model information sheets.
  • Oversight: Keep humans in the loop for high‑stakes decisions; enable challenge/appeal.
  • Capacity building: Train teachers/learners on ethical AI; run awareness workshops for parents.
  • Monitoring: Log decisions, track errors, and review models periodically.
